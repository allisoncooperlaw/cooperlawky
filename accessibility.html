Accessibility Statement

Cooper Law is committed to ensuring our website is accessible to all visitors, including those with disabilities. We believe access to legal information and services should not be limited by ability.

Our commitment

Cooper Law strives to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA, published by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). These guidelines are the international standard for web accessibility and explain how to make web content more accessible to people with a wide range of disabilities, including visual, auditory, motor, cognitive, and learning disabilities.

Estate planning is for everyone who loves someone — and our commitment to accessibility is part of that. Whether you use a screen reader, navigate by keyboard, rely on captioning, or use any other assistive technology, we want this site to work for you.

What we’ve done so far

Ongoing accessibility efforts include:

  • Semantic HTML structure with proper heading hierarchy
  • Descriptive alternative text on meaningful images
  • Labeled form fields and clear error messaging
  • Keyboard navigation support across interactive elements
  • A “skip to content” link at the top of every page for screen reader and keyboard users
  • Color contrast designed to meet WCAG AA standards for body text
  • ARIA labels on icon-only buttons and navigation landmarks
  • Responsive design that works across desktop, tablet, and mobile
  • Breadcrumb navigation on interior pages for orientation
  • Plain-English content written to be readable without specialized legal vocabulary

Known gaps and ongoing work

Accessibility is an ongoing process, not a finished destination. We are actively working to:

  • Audit and improve color contrast in all interface elements (not just body text)
  • Add captions and transcripts to any video content we publish in the future
  • Ensure all PDF documents we share are accessible (tagged, with proper reading order)
  • Continue testing with screen reader and keyboard-only users
  • Improve accessibility of any future e-commerce or interactive tools we add to the site

Report an accessibility issue

We want to hear from you.

If you encounter any accessibility barrier on this site — a page that doesn’t work with your assistive technology, an element you can’t reach by keyboard, an image without alt text, or anything else — we want to know about it. Your feedback helps us improve.

Email allison@cooperlawky.com with the subject line “Website Accessibility Issue” and include:

  • The page URL where you encountered the issue
  • A brief description of the problem
  • The assistive technology you’re using (if applicable)
  • Your preferred way to be contacted with our response

Or call 502-754-1351 — we will work with you to provide the information, service, or transaction you’re trying to access by an alternative method if needed.

Alternative ways to access services

If any part of this website is not accessible to you, we will work with you to provide the information or service through an alternative means — including phone, email, mailed documents, or in-person assistance for Louisville-area clients. No one should be excluded from access to legal services because of a website limitation.

Third-party content

This website uses some third-party services (such as form submission and analytics). We do our best to ensure these services are accessible, but we cannot control every aspect of third-party content. If you encounter accessibility issues with a third-party feature, please let us know and we will work to find a solution or alternative.
