About Allison

A fourth-generation Kentucky attorney — with a few extra letters after her name.

I come from a family of Kentucky lawyers. My great-grandfather practiced here. My grandfather practiced here. My father practiced here. When I passed the bar in 2015, I became the fourth generation to do the same — and then I kept going. I was admitted in Ohio in 2016 and Indiana in 2017, because estate planning doesn’t stop at state lines and neither should your attorney.

Before I built Cooper Law, I worked across several areas of practice that most estate planning attorneys never touch. I’ve worked in contract litigation, creditors’ rights, bankruptcy, wealth management, tax, and insurance. That range wasn’t an accident — it gave me something that’s hard to get any other way: a real understanding of what happens when plans fail, when families fight over assets, when debt complicates an inheritance, and when a will or trust wasn’t built to handle real life.

I started Cooper Law because I wanted to work on the other side of that equation — helping families build the plan before the crisis, not clean up after one. Estate planning sits at the intersection of everything I care about: legal strategy, tax planning, and financial security. With a J.D., CPA, and CFP®, I can speak all the different languages that make up a complete estate plan. Most attorneys speak one. Most financial advisors speak another. Your family’s plan needs someone who speaks all of them at the same time.

I’m not a replacement for your existing advisors. But because I understand what each of them does — from the legal structure to the tax implications to the financial strategy — I can build an estate plan that doesn’t contradict or ignore the rest of your financial life.

The difference it makes

Three credentials. One plan that actually works.

Most people work with a separate attorney, CPA, and financial advisor — and hope those three people are talking to each other. At Cooper Law, all three perspectives are already in the room.

Your attorney sees the legal picture

Who gets what. How assets transfer. Whether your documents hold up in court. What happens to your minor children. The formal legal structure of your plan.

Your CPA sees the tax picture

How assets are titled. Estate tax implications. Income tax considerations at death. Whether the structure of your plan creates unintended tax consequences.

Your CFP® sees the financial picture

How your retirement accounts fit in. Whether beneficiary designations align with your plan. How wealth transfers to the next generation efficiently.

When one person holds all three lenses, the gaps disappear. You get a plan where the legal structure, the tax strategy, and the financial picture all speak the same language — because they were designed together.
