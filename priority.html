Why this can’t wait

What happens if you go in without documents?

Without the right paperwork in place, the people closest to you may not be able to help you when it matters most.

Your spouse can’t make medical decisions

Without a medical power of attorney, hospitals may not let your spouse or family authorize treatment, access your medical records, or speak to your doctors — even in an emergency.

Your family can’t access your accounts

Without a durable power of attorney, nobody can pay your mortgage, access your bank accounts, or manage your finances while you’re incapacitated. Bills don’t stop because you’re in surgery.

A court decides what happens to your children

Without a will that names a guardian, the court appoints one. That process takes weeks, costs thousands, and the person chosen may not be who you would have picked.

The cost of not having these documents isn’t the $2,500 you didn’t spend. It’s the emergency guardianship hearing ($5,000+), the frozen bank accounts, the medical decisions made by strangers, and the months your family spends in probate court sorting out what you could have handled in 48 hours.
