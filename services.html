A note on online estate planning services

You may have seen websites offering wills and trusts for a few hundred dollars. Those services use generic templates and automated questionnaires — they do not provide legal advice, they cannot tailor documents to your state’s specific requirements, and they cannot coordinate your estate plan with your tax situation or financial goals.

In my experience reviewing documents from online providers, I have seen documents that did not comply with Kentucky’s witness and execution requirements, trusts that clients believed were funded when they were not, and powers of attorney drafted so narrowly that the agent could not actually do what the client intended. These are not hypothetical risks — they are problems I have seen real families face.

An estate plan is only as good as the attorney behind it. When you work with Cooper Law, you work directly with an attorney who holds a JD, CPA, and CFP® — which means your plan accounts for the legal, tax, and financial picture that an online template simply cannot see.
